Threats to Our Oceans

Plastic Pollution

Over 8 million tons of plastic enter our oceans every year, threatening marine life from plankton to whales.

Overfishing

Nearly 90% of the world's marine fish stocks are now fully exploited or overfished, disrupting the food chain.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures cause coral bleaching and ocean acidification, destroying vital habitats for thousands of species.

How You Can Help

  • Reduce Single-Use Plastics: Say no to plastic straws, bags, and water bottles.
  • Choose Sustainable Seafood: Support fisheries that maintain healthy ocean populations.
  • Lower Your Carbon Footprint: Reduce energy use to help slow ocean warming and acidification.
  • Participate in Cleanups: Join local beach or river cleaning events to prevent waste from reaching the sea.

Sea Turtles

The Ancient Voyagers: Out of 7 sea turtle species, 6 are classified as threatened or endangered.

  • Loss of nesting beaches due to development.
  • Entanglement in "ghost" fishing nets.
  • Mistaking plastic bags for jellyfish (their food).

Coral Reefs

The Rainforests of the Sea: Occupying less than 1% of the ocean floor, they support 25% of all marine life.

  • Coral bleaching caused by warming waters.
  • Damage from unsustainable tourism.
  • Providing critical coastal protection for humans.
üêã

Whales

The Ocean Giants: Whales help circulate nutrients and capture carbon, acting as natural climate engineers.

  • Noise pollution disrupting their communication.
  • Fatal collisions with large shipping vessels.
  • Chemical pollutants accumulating in their bodies.

How You Can Help These Species

  • For Turtles: Turn off lights near beaches during nesting season and keep plastic away from the shore.
  • For Corals: Wear reef-safe sunscreen and never touch or step on coral while diving.
  • For Whales: Support whale-watching companies that follow strict ethical and distance guidelines.
  • For All: Support Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) which provide safe havens for these animals to recover.
